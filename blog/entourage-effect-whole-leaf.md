  <h2 style="color: #D4AF37; font-family: 'Cinzel'; margin-top: 3rem;">The Safety Mechanism</h2>
  
  <p style="color: rgba(255,255,255,0.9); line-height: 1.8;">
    Here's the critical part: <strong>Corynantheidine is an opioid antagonist</strong>. It blocks some opioid receptor activity even as mitragynine activates others. This creates a <strong>natural ceiling effect</strong>—preventing the respiratory depression that kills with pure opioids.
  </p>
  
  <p style="color: rgba(255,255,255,0.9); line-height: 1.8;">
    Extracts that isolate mitragynine or synthesize 7-OH remove this safety brake. The result: higher overdose risk, faster tolerance, worse withdrawal.
  </p>

  <div style="background: rgba(255,0,110,0.1); border: 1px solid rgba(255,0,110,0.3); padding: 1.5rem; border-radius: 8px; margin: 2rem 0;">
    <strong style="color: #ff006e;">The "Enhanced" Trap</strong>
    <p style="color: rgba(255,255,255,0.9); margin-top: 0.5rem;">
      "Enhanced" kratom (leaf sprayed with extract) seems like best of both worlds. It's actually the worst—unpredictable alkaloid ratios, high 7-OH content, rapid dependence. Avoid any product labeled "enhanced," "super," or "XX% extract."
    </p>
  </div>

  <h2 style="color: #D4AF37; font-family: 'Cinzel'; margin-top: 3rem;">Research Evidence</h2>
  
  <p style="color: rgba(255,255,255,0.9); line-height: 1.8;">
    Kruegel et al. (2016) demonstrated that <strong>pure mitragynine</strong> has different receptor binding than <strong>whole alkaloid extract</strong>. The full spectrum creates "biased agonism"—activating pain relief pathways while avoiding respiratory depression pathways.
  </p>
  
  <p style="color: rgba(255,255,255,0.9); line-height: 1.8;">
    Isolated compounds lose this selectivity.
  </p>

  <div style="background: rgba(0,255,127,0.1); border: 1px solid rgba(0,255,127,0.3); padding: 1.5rem; border-radius: 8px; margin: 3rem 0;">
    <strong style="color: #00ff7f;">The Takeaway</strong>
    <p style="color: rgba(255,255,255,0.9); margin-top: 0.5rem;">

    </p>
