<div style="max-width: 800px; margin: 0 auto; padding: 2rem;">
  <h1 style="font-family: 'Cinzel', serif; color: #D4AF37;">FDA Import Alert 54-15</h1>
  
  <p style="color: rgba(255,255,255,0.6); font-size: 0.9rem; margin-bottom: 2rem;">Red List Expansion, 2022-Present</p>
  
  <p style="color: rgba(255,255,255,0.9); font-size: 1.1rem; line-height: 1.8; margin: 2rem 0;">
    In February 2022, the FDA expanded Import Alert 54-15 to cover all kratom products. This "Red List" designation allows detention without physical examination—and it's reshaping the American kratom supply chain.
  </p>

  <h2 style="color: #D4AF37; font-family: 'Cinzel'; margin-top: 3rem;">What Is a Red List Import Alert?</h2>
  
  <p style="color: rgba(255,255,255,0.9); line-height: 1.8;">
    Import Alerts are FDA enforcement tools. <strong>Red List</strong> means:
  </p>

  <ul style="color: rgba(255,255,255,0.9); line-height: 2;">
    <li>All shipments from listed manufacturers/exporters are subject to <strong>Detention Without Physical Examination (DWPE)</strong></li>
    <li>Product can be held at port indefinitely</li>
    <li>Vendor must provide independent lab testing to "green list" the shipment</li>
    <li>Costs $10,000-$50,000 per detention in storage and testing fees</li>
  </ul>

  <h2 style="color: #D4AF37; font-family: 'Cinzel'; margin-top: 3rem;">Why Kratom Was Added</h2>
  
  <p style="color: rgba(255,255,255,0.9); line-height: 1.8;">
    The FDA cites:
  </p>

  <ul style="color: rgba(255,255,255,0.9); line-height: 2;">
    <li>Salmonella contamination (2018 outbreak linked to 199 cases)</li>
    <li>Heavy metals (lead, nickel)</li>
    <li>Unapproved new drug claims (vendors marketing for specific conditions)</li>
    <li>Adulteration with O-desmethyltramadol (historical, not current)</li>
  </ul>

  <div style="background: rgba(255,0,110,0.1); border: 1px solid rgba(255,0,110,0.3); padding: 1.5rem; border-radius: 8px; margin: 2rem 0;">
    <strong style="color: #ff006e;">Impact on Supply Chains</strong>
    <p style="color: rgba(255,255,255,0.9); margin-top: 0.5rem;">
      Small vendors without legal teams have exited the market. Remaining vendors use "workarounds"—importing as "incense" or "research material" (legally risky), or paying premium prices for GMP-certified Indonesian suppliers who can provide FDA-acceptable documentation.
    </p>
  </div>

  <h2 style="color: #D4AF37; font-family: 'Cinzel'; margin-top: 3rem;">What Consumers Should Know</h2>
  
  <ul style="color: rgba(255,255,255,0.9); line-height: 2;">
    <li><strong>Stock issues:</strong> Vendor outages often trace to detained shipments</li>
    <li><strong>Price increases:</strong> Compliance costs passed to consumers</li>
    <li><strong>Quality variance:</strong> Desperate vendors may source untested material</li>
    <li><strong>The KCPA solution:</strong> State-level regulation provides framework for FDA compliance</li>
  </ul>

  <div style="background: rgba(0,255,127,0.1); border: 1px solid rgba(0,255,127,0.3); padding: 1.5rem; border-radius: 8px; margin: 3rem 0;">
    <strong style="color: #00ff7f;">The Path Forward</strong>
    <p style="color: rgba(255,255,255,0.9); margin-top: 0.5rem;">
      The AKA is working with FDA to establish a "green channel" for GMP-certified kratom—similar to the system for dietary supplements. Support the <a href="{{ '/blog/kratom-consumer-protection-act-status' | relative_url }}" style="color: #D4AF37;">KCPA</a> to create regulated pathways for safe importation.
    </p>
