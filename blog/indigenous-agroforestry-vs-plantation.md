  <h1 style="font-family: 'Cinzel', serif; color: #D4AF37;">Indigenous Agroforestry vs. Plantation Farming</h1>
  
  <p style="color: rgba(255,255,255,0.9); font-size: 1.1rem; line-height: 1.8; margin: 2rem 0;">
    Not all kratom is grown equal. The method of cultivation—wild agroforestry versus industrial monoculture—determines alkaloid quality, environmental sustainability, and farmer livelihoods.
  </p>

  <h2 style="color: #D4AF37; font-family: 'Cinzel'; margin-top: 3rem;">Traditional Agroforestry</h2>
  
  <p style="color: rgba(255,255,255,0.9); line-height: 1.8;">
    In Borneo and Sumatra, <em>Mitragyna speciosa</em> grows wild in <strong>mixed forest ecosystems</strong>:
  </p>

  <ul style="color: rgba(255,255,255,0.9); line-height: 2;">
    <li>Trees 15-30 years old, 50-80 feet tall</li>
    <li>Natural biodiversity—no pesticides or fertilizers</li>
    <li>Selective harvesting of mature leaves (higher alkaloid content)</li>
    <li>Integrated with rubber, fruit trees, and medicinal plants</li>
    <li>Family-managed plots passed through generations</li>
  </ul>

  <div style="background: rgba(0,255,127,0.1); border: 1px solid rgba(0,255,127,0.3); padding: 1.5rem; border-radius: 8px; margin: 2rem 0;">
    <strong style="color: #00ff7f;">The Alkaloid Advantage</strong>
    <p style="color: rgba(255,255,255,0.9); margin-top: 0.5rem;">
      Older trees in competitive forest environments produce more mitragynine as a defense compound. Wild-harvested leaf often tests at 2.0-2.5% mitragynine vs. 1.0-1.5% for plantation leaf.
    </p>
  </div>

  <h2 style="color: #D4AF37; font-family: 'Cinzel'; margin-top: 3rem;">Industrial Plantation Model</h2>
  
  <p style="color: rgba(255,255,255,0.9); line-height: 1.8;">
    Rising demand has driven monoculture conversion:
  </p>

  <ul style="color: rgba(255,255,255,0.9); line-height: 2;">
    <li>Clear-cut forest, single-species planting</li>
    <li>Trees harvested at 2-3 years (immature, weak alkaloids)</li>
    <li>Chemical fertilizers to force growth</li>
    <li>Pesticide use (kratom is susceptible to pests in monoculture)</li>
    <li>Wage labor replacing family ownership</li>
  </ul>

  </p>

  <ul style="color: rgba(255,255,255,0.9); line-height: 2;">

    <li>Mature tree harvesting only (10+ years)</li>
    <li>Organic certification (no synthetic inputs)</li>
