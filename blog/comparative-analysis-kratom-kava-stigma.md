  <h2 style="color: #D4AF37; font-family: 'Cinzel'; margin-top: 3rem;">Why Kava Escaped Scrutiny</h2>
  
  <p style="color: rgba(255,255,255,0.9); line-height: 1.8;">
    <strong>1. The "Supplement" Pathway:</strong> Kava entered the US market through health food stores as a "natural anti-anxiety" supplement before FDA scrutiny intensified. By the time liver toxicity concerns emerged (2001), the industry was established.
  </p>
  
  <p style="color: rgba(255,255,255,0.9); line-height: 1.8;">
    <strong>2. No Opioid Association:</strong> Kava's mechanism (GABA modulation) doesn't trigger the same regulatory alarm as kratom's opioid receptor activity—despite both having abuse potential.
  </p>
  
  <p style="color: rgba(255,255,255,0.9); line-height: 1.8;">
    <strong>3. Cultural Branding:</strong> Kava is marketed as "island relaxation"—tiki bars, beach vibes, vacation. Kratom is associated with "opioid epidemic" and "addiction" in media narratives.
  </p>

  <h2 style="color: #D4AF37; font-family: 'Cinzel'; margin-top: 3rem;">The Irony</h2>
  
  <p style="color: rgba(255,255,255,0.9); line-height: 1.8;">
    Kava actually has a <strong>worse safety record</strong> than kratom—over 100 cases of liver toxicity, some fatal, led to bans in Europe and Canada. Kratom has no confirmed deaths from pure leaf, yet faces stricter regulation.
  </p>
  
  <p style="color: rgba(255,255,255,0.9); line-height: 1.8;">
    The difference: <strong>timing and framing</strong>. Kratom arrived during the opioid crisis, making it a target for "drug war" logic. Kava arrived during the "natural supplement" boom of the 1990s.
  </p>

  <h2 style="color: #D4AF37; font-family: 'Cinzel'; margin-top: 3rem;">Lessons for Kratom Advocacy</h2>
  
  <p style="color: rgba(255,255,255,0.9); line-height: 1.8;">
    The kratom community can learn from kava's trajectory:
  </p>

  <ul style="color: rgba(255,255,255,0.9); line-height: 2;">
    <li><strong>Emphasize traditional use:</strong> Frame as "Southeast Asian wellness practice" not "legal high"</li>
    <li><strong>Promote social venues:</strong> Kava bars normalized consumption; kratom cafes can do the same</li>
    <li><strong>Quality control:</strong> Kava's liver issues stemmed from poor extraction methods; kratom must avoid similar mistakes through GMP</li>
    <li><strong>Regulatory engagement:</strong> Kava industry worked with FDA to establish safety standards; kratom must do the same via <a href="{{ '/blog/kratom-consumer-protection-act-status' | relative_url }}" style="color: #D4AF37;">KCPA</a></li>
  </ul>
