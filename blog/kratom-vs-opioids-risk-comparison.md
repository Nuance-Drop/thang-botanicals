  <h1 style="font-family: 'Cinzel', serif; color: #D4AF37;">Kratom vs. Prescription Opioids</h1>
  
  <p style="color: rgba(255,255,255,0.9); font-size: 1.1rem; line-height: 1.8; margin: 2rem 0;">
    The opioid crisis killed 80,000 Americans in 2022. Kratom is used by millions as an alternative. How do the risks actually compare?
  </p>

  <h2 style="color: #D4AF37; font-family: 'Cinzel'; margin-top: 3rem;">Respiratory Depression: The Killer Difference</h2>
  
  <p style="color: rgba(255,255,255,0.9); line-height: 1.8;">
    <strong>Opioid overdose deaths are caused by respiratory depression</strong>—the brain stops signaling the lungs to breathe. This happens because full opioid agonists (morphine, fentanyl, oxycodone) activate beta-arrestin pathways.
  </p>
  
  <p style="color: rgba(255,255,255,0.9); line-height: 1.8;">
    Kratom's mitragynine is a <strong>partial agonist with biased signaling</strong>. It preferentially activates G-protein pathways (pain relief) over beta-arrestin (respiratory depression). The result: <strong>analgesia without the lethal side effect</strong>.
  </p>

  <div style="background: rgba(0,245,212,0.1); border: 1px solid rgba(0,245,212,0.3); padding: 1.5rem; border-radius: 8px; margin: 2rem 0;">
    <strong style="color: #00f5d4;">The Data</strong>
    <p style="color: rgba(255,255,255,0.9); margin-top: 0.5rem;">
      In preclinical studies, rats given massive doses of mitragynine showed <strong>no respiratory depression</strong>. Rats given equivalent doses of morphine stopped breathing.
    </p>
  </div>

  <h2 style="color: #D4AF37; font-family: 'Cinzel'; margin-top: 3rem;">Overdose Potential</h2>
  
  <p style="color: rgba(255,255,255,0.9); line-height: 1.8;">
    <strong>Prescription opioids:</strong> Narrow therapeutic index. Dose escalation common. Lethal overdose risk increases with tolerance.
  </p>
  
  <p style="color: rgba(255,255,255,0.9); line-height: 1.8;">
    <strong>Kratom:</strong> Ceiling effect on respiratory depression. "Wobbles" (nystagmus/nausea) occur before dangerous doses, creating natural deterrent. <strong>No confirmed deaths from kratom alone</strong> in forensic literature.
  </p>

  <div style="background: rgba(255,193,7,0.1); border: 1px solid rgba(255,193,7,0.3); padding: 1.5rem; border-radius: 8px; margin: 2rem 0;">
    <strong style="color: #ffc107;">Important Caveat</strong>
    <p style="color: rgba(255,255,255,0.9); margin-top: 0.5rem;">
      Kratom is <strong>not risk-free</strong>. Dependence, withdrawal, and rare liver injury are documented. But the risk profile is closer to caffeine than to oxycodone. For chronic pain patients, it may represent the lesser of two evils.
    </p>
  </div>
