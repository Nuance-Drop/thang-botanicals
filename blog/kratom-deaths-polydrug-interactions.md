  <h1 style="font-family: 'Cinzel', serif; color: #D4AF37;">The Truth About "Kratom-Related Deaths"</h1>
  
  <p style="color: rgba(255,255,255,0.9); font-size: 1.1rem; line-height: 1.8; margin: 2rem 0;">
    The FDA claims 44 "kratom-associated deaths." But when you read the actual autopsy reports, a different story emerges.
  </p>

  <h2 style="color: #D4AF37; font-family: 'Cinzel'; margin-top: 3rem;">The Polydrug Pattern</h2>
  
  <p style="color: rgba(255,255,255,0.9); line-height: 1.8;">
    In <strong>43 of 44 cases</strong>, the deceased had multiple substances in their system:
  </p>

  <ul style="color: rgba(255,255,255,0.9); line-height: 2;">
    <li>Fentanyl (most common)</li>
    <li>Heroin</li>
    <li>Benzodiazepines (Xanax, etc.)</li>
    <li>Prescription opioids</li>
    <li>Cocaine</li>
    <li>Alcohol (often in lethal amounts)</li>
  </ul>

  <div style="background: rgba(0,245,212,0.1); border: 1px solid rgba(0,245,212,0.3); padding: 1.5rem; border-radius: 8px; margin: 2rem 0;">
    <strong style="color: #00f5d4;">The One "Pure" Kratom Case</strong>
    <p style="color: rgba(255,255,255,0.9); margin-top: 0.5rem;">
      The single death attributed solely to kratom involved a product adulterated with <strong>O-desmethyltramadol</strong> (synthetic opioid). Pure leaf has never been confirmed as the sole cause of death in forensic literature.
    </p>
  </div>
