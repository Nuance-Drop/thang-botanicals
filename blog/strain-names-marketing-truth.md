<div style="max-width: 800px; margin: 0 auto; padding: 2rem;">
  <h1 style="font-family: 'Cinzel', serif; color: #D4AF37;">Why "Strain" Names Are Mostly Marketing</h1>
  
  <p style="color: rgba(255,255,255,0.9); font-size: 1.1rem; line-height: 1.8; margin: 2rem 0;">
    "Green Maeng Da" sounds exotic, but it's not a botanical strain. It's a <strong>drying method</strong>. Here's how post-harvest processing creates the effects you feel—and why the name on the bag matters less than you think.
  </p>

  <h2 style="color: #D4AF37; font-family: 'Cinzel'; margin-top: 3rem;">The Fermentation Process</h2>
  
  <p style="color: rgba(255,255,255,0.9); line-height: 1.8;">
    All kratom starts as green leaf. The "vein color" is determined by <strong>post-harvest processing</strong>, not genetics:
  </p>

  <ul style="color: rgba(255,255,255,0.9); line-height: 2; margin: 1.5rem 0;">
    <li><strong style="color: #00ff7f;">Green:</strong> Indoor drying, minimal oxidation. Preserves original alkaloid profile.</li>
    <li><strong style="color: #fff;">White:</strong> Extended indoor drying with UV light exposure. Higher mitragynine content.</li>
    <li><strong style="color: #ff006e;">Red:</strong> Fermented in bags or outdoor drying. Converts mitragynine to 7-OH (sedating).</li>
    <li><strong style="color: #FFD700;">Yellow/Gold:</strong> Hybrid process—dried then fermented briefly. Balanced profile.</li>
  </ul>

  <div style="background: rgba(255,0,110,0.1); border: 1px solid rgba(255,0,110,0.3); padding: 1.5rem; border-radius: 8px; margin: 2rem 0;">
    <strong style="color: #ff006e;">The "Maeng Da" Myth</strong>
    <p style="color: rgba(255,255,255,0.9); margin-top: 0.5rem;">
      "Maeng Da" (Thai for "pimp grade") is marketing, not a region. It typically indicates:
      <ul style="margin-top: 0.5rem;">
        <li>Older leaves (higher alkaloid concentration)</li>
        <li>Specific drying technique</li>
        <li>Often a blend of multiple harvests</li>
      </ul>
    </p>
  </div>

  <h2 style="color: #D4AF37; font-family: 'Cinzel'; margin-top: 3rem;">What Actually Matters</h2>
  
  <p style="color: rgba(255,255,255,0.9); line-height: 1.8;">
    Instead of chasing exotic names, demand:
  </p>

  <ol style="color: rgba(255,255,255,0.9); line-height: 1.8;">
    <li><strong>Lab Testing:</strong> Heavy metals (lead, cadmium, arsenic), salmonella, E. coli</li>
    <li><strong>Alkaloid Content:</strong> Mitragynine levels (1.5-2.5% is typical; >3% is potent)</li>
    <li><strong>GMP Certification:</strong> Good Manufacturing Practices compliance</li>
    <li><strong>Batch Numbers:</strong> Traceability for recalls or quality issues</li>
  </ol>

  <p style="color: rgba(255,255,255,0.6); margin-top: 3rem; font-size: 0.9rem;">
    Source: <a href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC5811370/" style="color: #D4AF37;">Kruegel et al., "Synthetic and Receptor Signaling Explorations of the Mitragyna Alkaloids"</a> (2016)
  </p>
